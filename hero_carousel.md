## The Visionary: Walt Disney
![Walt Disney](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/ed/Walt_disney_portrait.jpg)

Walt Disney's boundless imagination and pioneering spirit laid the foundation for an entertainment empire that would touch the hearts of millions across the globe.

<!-- slide -->
## The Beginning: Steamboat Willie
![Steamboat Willie](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4e/Steamboat-willie.jpg)

*Steamboat Willie* (1928) introduced the world to Mickey Mouse. It was one of the first cartoons with synchronized sound, revolutionizing the animation industry and putting Disney on the map.

<!-- slide -->
## The Gamble: Snow White
![Snow White Poster](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/4/49/Snow_White_1937_poster.png)

Released in 1937, *Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs* was dubbed "Disney's Folly" during production. It became the first full-length cel animated feature in history, proving critics wrong. Its massive success provided the capital to build the Burbank studios and established Disney as the undisputed leader in feature animation.

<!-- slide -->
## The Modern Era: Frozen
![Frozen Poster](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/05/Frozen_%282013_film%29_poster.jpg)

*Frozen* (2013) brought the classic Disney musical fairy tale into the modern era. Becoming a global cultural phenomenon, it demonstrated the enduring emotional power of Disney's storytelling.

<!-- slide -->
## Bringing Families Closer
![Family Magic](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1541781774459-bb2af2f05b55?q=80&w=2060&auto=format&fit=crop)

For nearly a century, Disney magic has been bringing families closer together. Through shared stories, unforgettable characters, and shared memories, the Disney legacy continues to create bonds that span generations, reminding us all of the child within.
